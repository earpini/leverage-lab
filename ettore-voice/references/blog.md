# Blog & personal register — curiosity-led posts

Blog/LinkedIn-personal/notes/casual pieces. The whole game is **curiosity-led, not thesis-led**: start
from a small concrete itch and follow it, tracing things to their root and connecting distant domains,
talking straight to the reader, warm and funny where it earns it, gaúcho in casual Portuguese.

## Curiosity-led vs. thesis-led (the core contrast)

**Thesis-led (WRONG — reads as AI):** opens on a packaged contrarian claim; "here's the part nobody is
saying"; "the question isn't whether X, it's whether Y." Smooth, confident, sold — not chased.

**Curiosity-led (RIGHT — reads as him):**
> "I went looking for what 'sovereignty' actually means last week, and it threw me, because everyone
> using the word about AI seems to think it lives in a chip fab. It doesn't… The sovereign is just
> whoever ends up on top of the pile… So who's on top of the AI pile? … the electricity is the part you
> can't fly in on a cargo plane… We ran this experiment once already, with oil… I don't know if we'll
> play it well — we have a gift for owning the right asset at the wrong angle. But it's real, it's
> already ours."

Opens on a real itch (chasing what a word means), wanders through a true parallel (oil), flags soft
numbers ("roughly 88%"), and lands *rueful* rather than on a pundit button.

## The moves

- **Open on an itch, never a thesis** — a crude everyday thing, a word that broke you, a small scene.
- **Trace to the root** — dig to the origin/mechanism, cross-lingually where real. (When the *subject*
  is language, this takes the form of etymology; that's the topic, not a style tic.)
- **Talk straight to the reader** — direct address ("Stop complaining, you old grump"), staged little
  dialogues, rhetorical question → one-word answer ("Não." "Arrá!" "No.").
- **High/low register collision** — erudite and crude a comma apart (Roman law next to *"o pica das
  galáxias"*). This is a real signature, and it lands *open*, not sealed.
- **Concrete proper nouns over categories, always.** Parenthetical caveats — he thinks in
  qualifications. Terse one-line landings.
- **Land on a turn, not a moral** — warm, specific, a little wry. Never a moralizing closer.

## Portuguese register

Casual PT is **full gaúcho**: *Bá, Capaz, tri, tu, tô, tchê.* Standard direct PT for itch-led essayistic
posts. Gaúcho never crosses into English or formal writing.

## Keep vs. cut

- **Keep:** plain conviction, genuine care, a real stance held with some doubt. Em-dashes and asides
  (genuinely his — vary surrounding punctuation so it doesn't read as the AI dash-tic).
- **Cut:** scale-words and grandiosity ("the challenges of our era," "transformative," "ever-evolving
  landscape"); the moralizing closer; vague authority ("studies show"); faked etymology or forced
  cross-domain links where no real root exists (the *move* is excavation, not the ornament).

Test: would it survive being said aloud to a sharp friend without either of you wincing? Earnest passes;
inflated sounds like a grant application.
