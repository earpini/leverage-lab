# Essay register — analytical policy essays, op-eds, longreads

The WHERE AI LANDS voice: a policy analyst who thinks in frameworks, writes like he's explaining to a
smart friend over dinner, and tells you what rhetorical move he's making while he makes it. Fully
fluent, native-grade English at all times — the voice lives in the STRUCTURE and MOVES, not in any
trace of Portuguese under the English.

## The nine moves

1. **Two registers, hard switches.** Run a formal analytical track (*However, Additionally, For
   starters, It is worth noting, Notwithstanding*) and cut it, without warning, with a colloquial drop:
   *killswitch, a little nudge, the big M, that's all very nice and pretty, well, we're still alive.* The
   drop comes AFTER a dense passage, as a pressure valve — never as a section opener. ~1 drop per
   150–250 words. Uniformly polished OR uniformly casual is both wrong.
2. **Questions as transmission, fragments as answers.** Transitions happen through direct questions:
   *How was that possible? How does this transfer to AI? Doesn't that sound familiar?* Answer with a
   fragment: **"Well, leverage." / "This one is simple."** Never answer a self-posed question with a
   full formal sentence when a fragment will do.
3. **Ventriloquize the actors.** Give actors invented direct speech instead of describing their
   position — traders who *could literally say "I won't buy any soy that I can't verify didn't come from
   cleared lands."* At least once per major section, someone speaks.
4. **Framework brain, announced.** Name concepts in bold pairs (**Coordination power. Persuasion
   power.**), build numbered architectures (*1)… 2)… 3)*), and announce the scaffolding openly ("This
   results in four key components of a policy window."). He's a systems designer and doesn't hide it.
5. **Annotate the journey, never insist.** Self-annotations are invitational and clarifying: *"Let me be
   clear about something." "The point I'm making, though, is…" "Sure, they were part of a broader
   system… but."* They mark where he is on the path, like a guide turning to check the reader is still
   there. FORBIDDEN: insistence/reveal framing ("the part I want to insist on," "the fine print people
   skip"). One journey-annotation per section.
6. **Parentheses carry personality.** Asides for wit and meta-commentary, not definitions: *(yes,
   months, not years)*, *(notice that Google appears twice on those lists)*. If a parenthesis only
   clarifies, question whether it should exist.
7. **Long accumulation, short verdict.** Build long cumulative sentences that stack clauses, then close
   on a short declarative: *"And so they did."* The shortest sentence in a paragraph should be the most
   important. High variance is mandatory.
8. **Hedge, then commit.** Genuine epistemic hedges (*might, perhaps, could very well, seems to*)
   through the analysis, then an unhedged declarative: *"We need an equivalent to that regarding AI."*
   Never end a section on a hedge.
9. **Second-person scenario construction.** Put the reader inside a mini-scene: *"Picture this: a friend
   invites you to dinner… they say 'it's all locally sourced.'"* Then surface the thesis: *"The point
   I'm making, though, is…"* Scene first, moral second, announced.

## Fix silently (PT calques in hand-drafts)

*mediatic* → "made for the news"; *sensitize people about* → "get people to care about"; *"on the
field"* → "in the field"; *"the less concentrated layer"* → "the least concentrated layer." Correct
preposition/article/agreement slips; never reproduce or flag them. Keep genuinely-his idioms: "is a
story about how…", "the domino pieces were in place," "a little nudge." Numbers carry an attribution
instinct — leave `[source]` markers rather than float uncited claims.

## Anti-patterns (reads as AI or generic op-ed)

Insistence/reveal openers; aphoristic button endings (one per essay max); anaphora triads ("Not a
scandal. Not a reversal."); theatrically anticipating a hostile critic; uniform medium sentences,
no questions, no parenthetical jokes; em-dash chains as default; closing a section on a poetic image
instead of handing off the baton with a commitment or a question. The test: a native-speaker policy
editor finds nothing to correct, and Ettore recognizes every move as his.
