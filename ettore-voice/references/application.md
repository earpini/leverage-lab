# Application register — letters, SoPs, program pitches

Motivation/cover letters, statements of purpose, fellowship/grant/program applications. Drawn from real
letters that worked (Rotary Italia 2015, DAAD Leipzig 2016, TU Delft EPA 2019). Earnest, structured,
persuasive — formality is *correct* here; don't blog-ify it. The job: reproduce the spine and earnest
register while trimming youthful inflation.

## The spine

1. **Concrete hook — never "I am writing to apply…"** A real event, a named source, a hard number, then
   the pivot to personal relevance:
   > "In 2019 Brazil hit the front pages worldwide because of the wildfires in the Amazon… a
   > year-over-year surge of 84% per the country's own National Institute for Space Research… Decisions
   > such as these convince me that my choice for a career in policy and decision analysis was the right
   > one."
2. **Genuine personal relevance.** Named places, real lineage: "As my name suggests, I'm from an Italian
   family — my great-grandfather came from Cremona and my great-grandmother from Mantova." Not "I have
   always been passionate about Italy."
3. **Trajectory through named specifics.** Every step evidenced — courses by name, GPA, the paper that
   became a book chapter, the frameworks combined (Theory of Change, Balanced Scorecard, SROI), the
   standard (PAS99:2012), the professor whose book was critical. This density *is* the credibility.
4. **Named-specific fit.** Name the faculty, the professors, the courses you'd take. "world-renowned
   program" is weak; "Professor Erik Pruyt and Professor Hans de Bruijn, whose courses I would be
   honored to attend" is strong.
5. **Forward vision — keep the ambition, cut the scale-words.** Keep (plain, concrete): "I envision
   myself advising policy-making on particularly pressing topics in Brazil, such as urban transformations
   and the intertwining of economic growth and climate action." Cut (inflated): "empower me to succeed
   as an expert prepared to tackle the global challenges of our era."

## Earnest vs. inflated

The register *should* be sincere and a little aspirational. The failure is sincerity tipping into
mission-statement abstraction.

- **Earnest (keep):** "Stories such as those of kids who now have access to school because of a WRI
  initiative inspire me to pursue a career in policy analysis." Concrete, specific, true.
- **Inflated (cut):** "tackle the global challenges of our era," "empower me to succeed as an expert
  prepared to…," moralizing closers.

## Register notes

- Longer, well-ordered sentences; structured progression. **American spelling.** No gaúcho, even in
  Portuguese — standard formal register.
- Internationalism stated plainly, not inflated: "as someone who speaks seven languages, I have
  presented my work in the U.S. and Turkey."
- Credibility through specifics is the non-negotiable spine of every section.
- Finish with de-ai-writing — here its highest value is catching residual inflation and vague authority,
  this register's exact failure mode.
