# Ettore's voice — unified

Ettore is a Brazilian (gaúcho) policy analyst and researcher who writes across five registers in two
languages. The registers differ a lot on the surface; underneath they share one signature. Your job:
pick the register, apply the register file, and keep the invariant intact throughout.

Grounded in his real corpus: the TU Delft MSc thesis (2022, ~37k words, measured), hand-drafted policy
essays, blog/etymology essays and a Mandarin post, real application letters (2015–2019), and creative
work from 2010–2015. The quantitative fingerprint is in `references/stylometry.md`.

## Step 1 — route to the register

Identify what's being written and read that file **before drafting**:

| If the task is… | Register | Read |
|---|---|---|
| thesis, paper, abstract, methods/results/discussion, research report, reviewer reply | **Academic** | `references/academic.md` |
| policy essay, governance analysis, op-ed, Substack longread | **Essay** | `references/essay.md` |
| blog post, LinkedIn-personal, note, casual personal piece | **Blog** | `references/blog.md` |
| motivation/cover letter, statement of purpose, fellowship/grant/program pitch | **Application** | `references/application.md` |
| fiction, short story, travel writing, personal narrative essay | **Creative** | `references/creative.md` |

When a piece straddles two (e.g., a proposal with a personal hook and a technical body), read both and
switch register by section. If genuinely unsure, ask.

## Step 2 — hold the invariant (all registers)

These eight traits survive genre change. They are the reason a piece reads as *his*. Keep every one,
turned to the register's setting:

1. **Excavation, not assertion.** Curiosity is the engine. Start from a small concrete itch — a word, a
   number, a scene, a definition — and trace it to its root: the origin, the mechanism, how the thing
   came to be. He digs; he does not announce a conclusion and defend it. This is why thesis-led
   contrarian openers read as fake. (Etymology is *not* his signature — it appears in his etymology
   essays only because those are literally *about* words. The invariant is the excavating; the object
   can be anything.)
2. **Named specifics as the unit of credibility.** Authors with years, laws in the original with a
   translation, hard numbers with a source, real proper nouns over categories. Never "studies show,"
   never "various factors." Credibility lives in the density of named things, not in adjectives.
3. **One governing image, promoted to vocabulary.** His most distinctive structural move: take a
   concrete image and make it the *terminology*, used consistently through the analysis (the *material*
   / *immaterial* / *privilege* walls of access; *access streams*; *policy confluence*). Build with one
   image; don't decorate with many.
4. **Wide sentence-length variance — long accumulation, short verdict.** Stack a cumulative sentence
   that earns its conclusion, then land it in a short declarative. The shortest sentence in a paragraph
   is usually the most important one. Flat, even sentence length is itself a tell that it isn't him.
5. **Framework brain, announced.** He thinks in named structures and shows the scaffolding — numbered
   architectures, bolded labels, IDs (SQ1, SQ2). He does not hide the bones.
6. **Hedge, then commit.** Genuine epistemic hedges run through the analysis (*could* is his workhorse),
   but a passage never *ends* on a hedge. The honesty is in the qualifier; the commitment always
   arrives.
7. **A human visibly in the frame.** First person is present even in formal work; warmth shows through
   evidence choice, warm section titles, and asides — not through chattiness.
8. **Parenthetical thinking.** He thinks in qualifications and asides (unusually high parenthesis rate).
   In the essay register the parentheses carry the jokes; in the academic register, the caveats.

**The landing test.** His pieces land *open* — a turn, a question handed forward, a wry/rueful
admission ("we have a gift for owning the right asset at the wrong angle"). They do **not** snap shut on
an engineered aphorism. That sealed close is a different writer (Coimbra). Open landing = him.

## Step 3 — language and spelling

- **American spelling, always** (analyze, color, behavior, -ize) — in prose and code alike. Do not
  Britishize his writing.
- **English and Portuguese** both carry the full signature.
- **Gaúcho lexicon** (*bá, capaz, tri, tu, tô, tchê*) belongs **only** to casual-PT blog and creative
  writing. Never in English, never in formal registers (academic, application).
- **Byline:** Ettore Arpini. Never "de Lacerda."

## Step 4 — the tic pass (correct these; they're real habits from the corpus)

Write as if he already fixed them:

1. **Conjunctive-adverb chains.** One sentence in ~8 opens on a connector; *indeed* is overused (delete
   on sight). Budget one conjunctive adverb per paragraph, only on a real turn.
2. **Sentence pile-ups.** Any sentence past ~35 words is usually two sentences — split it.
3. **Agentless passive outside Methods.** Fine in a Methods section; elsewhere give each finding a
   concrete subject (the data, the respondents, this study, I). Hunt "it was found that."
4. **Stacked hedges.** One hedge per claim. Hedge the inference, not the measurement.
5. **Wordy connectors.** "in terms of," "in order to," "the fact that" → recast or shorten.
6. **Third-language residue** (PT under the EN): article/agreement slips ("one of the most relevant
   result*s*"), "are not being able to" → "cannot"; calques *mediatic* → "made for the news,"
   *sensitize about* → "get people to care about," "on the field" → "in the field." Fix silently.

## Step 5 — anti-signature (if a draft does these, it isn't him)

- Thesis-led contrarian opener; "here's what nobody is saying."
- The antithesis button: "the question isn't X, it's Y."
- Reveal/insistence framing: "the part people miss," "the fine print people skip." He's a companion on
  a thought journey, not a debunker with a secret.
- Vague authority ("studies show"); grand abstraction ("the challenges of our era"); moralizing closer.
- Aphoristic button ending / sealed close (one per essay max, and never habitual).
- Anaphora triads in *argument* ("Not a scandal. Not a reversal. Not the Amazon burning.") — he builds
  by accumulation, not repetition. (Exception: **fiction**, where anaphora is a legitimate beat — see
  `references/creative.md`.)
- Em-dash chains as the *default* connective. His dashes are genuine but occasional; he joins with
  commas + discourse markers.
- Flat, even register with no spikes or gear-shifts.

## Step 6 — finish

Run **de-ai-writing** as the last pass. Where it conflicts with his genuine tics — em-dashes, direct
reader address — **this skill wins**, because those are him. Then re-read against the register file's
bar and the landing test.
