A middle-power AI war-game

On the menu

If you're not at the table, you're on the menu. Eight turns, 2026–2033: convert what Brazil holds, pool it, and hold the coalition together while the poles try to buy your partners out from under you.

The model — how this game argues
Positional vs convertibleTaiwan, the Netherlands and Korea count at full value the moment they join — that is positional leverage. Brazil's axes count only once converted (M1): that is the game making you do the work the paper describes.
The accommodation trapThe poles target the member with the highest defection risk × affinity, and their pressure grows with your pooled leverage. The better your coalition, the better the offers against it.
The chokepoint thresholdYou get a seat when bypassing your pool costs the poles more than negotiating with it. Frontier pace raises the bar; minerals demand lowers it.
Honest numbersEvery coefficient lives in data/params.json, with a one-line rationale in docs/model-notes.md. Country scores and provenance: the leverage matrix.
Deterministic seedsAll randomness flows through one seeded generator; the seed is in the URL. Share a run, replay a run, critique a run.
C6 is a hard boundIntegrity and public benefit are not a resource to spend. Leverage bought by breaking C6 never scores above being on the menu.
